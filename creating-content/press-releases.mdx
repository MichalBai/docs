## What is a press release?

A press release is a formal written announcement shared with media outlets about significant business events — such as product launches, company milestones, funding rounds, partnership announcements, or other newsworthy updates. Unlike blog posts or social content, press releases follow a third-person, journalistic format and are distributed directly to journalists and news sites.

## Distribution guidelines

Your press release must meet the following standards to be accepted and distributed:

- **Must be newsworthy** — product launches, funding rounds, partnerships, company milestones, or awards qualify; purely promotional content does not
- **Recommended length: 300–600 words** — longer releases up to 1,600 words are accepted if the content remains clear and relevant
- **No promotional or sales language** — avoid phrases like "FREE", "Don't miss this opportunity!", or anything that reads like an advertisement
- **Must be written in third-person** — first-person and second-person narratives are not accepted
- **Must include factual, verifiable information** — unsubstantiated health claims, financial promises, or get-rich-quick schemes are prohibited
- **Complete media contact required** — include a verifiable name and email address
- **No prohibited content** — personal attacks, defamatory content, celebrity mentions without authorization, or content designed to deceive are automatically disqualified
