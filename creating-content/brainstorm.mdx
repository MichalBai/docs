Brainstorm is a conversational creation mode where you think through ideas with Pressmaster before committing to a final piece of content. Use it to explore a topic, research angles, run an interview, and develop your thinking — then turn the output into one or more posts.

## When to use Brainstorm

- You have a topic in mind but haven't shaped it into a clear angle yet
- You want to run an interview before writing
- You want to explore multiple angles on the same subject
- You want to research a trend or idea before deciding how to approach it

## How it works

Open Brainstorm from the **+ Create** menu. You'll enter a chat interface where you can:

- **Start with an idea** — type what's on your mind and Pressmaster will help you explore and refine it
- **Run an interview** — let Pressmaster ask you targeted questions about your topic to extract your best thinking
- **Analyze and research** — ask Pressmaster to research a subject, find supporting data, or suggest angles based on your expertise

At any point in the conversation, you can turn what you've developed into content — a single post, a series, or a full plan.

## Interview styles

When running an interview inside Brainstorm, you can choose how the conversation flows:

- **Adaptive** — Questions are asked one at a time, each building on your previous answer. Recommended for most users.
- **Conversational** — A free-flowing dialogue where you speak directly with Pressmaster, like talking to a journalist.
- **Voice** — Speak your answers out loud; Pressmaster transcribes and extracts your best ideas.

## Creating content from Brainstorm

Once you've developed your idea, select the output you want to create:

- **Single post** — move into Instant Creation with your brainstorm as context
- **Content series** — carry your ideas into Plan Ahead

<Tip>
  If your Content Twin has a score above 100, Brainstorm sessions will draw on your existing thinking — making the output feel more like you from the start.
</Tip>
