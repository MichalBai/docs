Your Brand Voice profile teaches Pressmaster how you write — your tone, structure, rhythm, and personality. Once set up, every piece of content Pressmaster generates applies your signature voice automatically, without you having to re-specify it for each new piece.

## What Brand Voice captures

When you create a Brand Voice, Pressmaster analyzes your writing sample and extracts:

- **Tone** — professional, conversational, authoritative, casual, or any blend
- **Sentence structure and rhythm** — short punchy sentences vs. longer, flowing prose
- **Vocabulary and phrasing preferences** — the specific words and expressions you reach for
- **Level of formality** — how you address your audience and frame your ideas

## Creating a Brand Voice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Brand Voice">
    Open **Settings** from the left menu, then select **Brand Guides**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Add Brand Voice'">
    You'll find this option in the Brand Voice section, after completing your general Brand Guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide a writing sample">
    Paste a piece of content you've written — a social post, article, or any text that sounds like you. If you don't have an existing sample, you can paste a post from a creator in your industry whose style you want to use as a starting framework.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the analysis">
    Click **Start Analyzing**. Pressmaster examines your tone, sentence structure, narrative approach, and other communication patterns. The analysis takes a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and customize the result">
    Review the generated Brand Voice profile. You can adjust any of the detected attributes and add your own custom instructions. For improved formatting accuracy, scroll to the bottom of the results and add: "Always follow the following structure" — then paste a complete example post below it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name and save your Brand Voice">
    Give the profile a descriptive name (for example: "LinkedIn posts" or "Long-form articles"), then click **Apply Brand Voice** to save it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multiple Brand Voices

You can create separate Brand Voice profiles for different content types or different clients. Available Brand Voices per plan:

| Plan | Brand Voices |
|---|---|
| Starter | 1 |
| Pro | 2 |
| Leader | Unlimited |
| Agency plans | Unlimited |

Use multiple voices for different contexts — for example, a formal voice for long-form articles and a casual voice for social media posts. Agencies can create a separate Brand Voice for each client.

When creating content, select the appropriate Brand Voice from the dropdown menu on the content creation screen.

## Editing your Brand Voice

To update an existing Brand Voice profile, go to **Account Settings > Brand Guide** at any time. You can adjust the analyzed attributes, add new instructions, or re-run the analysis with a fresh writing sample.

## Brand Guide vs. Brand Voice

These two settings work together but serve distinct purposes:

| | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Brand Guide** | Your mission, target audience, positioning, and company overview — the strategic context Pressmaster uses to understand your brand |
| **Brand Voice** | Your writing style and tone — the tactical voice Pressmaster applies when generating content |

Together, your Brand Guide and Brand Voice form your complete brand profile. The Brand Guide tells Pressmaster *what* to say; the Brand Voice tells it *how* to say it.

## Language settings

Pressmaster generates content in the language you set in **Settings > Account Settings > Default Language**. Supported languages currently include UK English, American English, and German. Changing your language setting affects all content creation, including interviews, articles, and social media posts.

<Tip>
  Your Brand Guide shows "in progress" if you haven't answered every question — that's fine. Pressmaster uses whatever information you've already provided. Fill in more detail over time for increasingly personalized results.
</Tip>
